About me

/əˈbaʊt/ /miː/

I'm a tech guy. I've been playing with computers since my early life, and with GNU/Linux since I was nine years-old. I always try my best in learning something new when trying to resolve a problem. Most specifically, I like not only finding the how behind the solution, but also the why.

I like to keep things simple and avoid overcomplicated logics that have too much going on, and rather prefer going for the cleanest alternative. This applies in many fields; I am really picky regarding my code style and profile appearance; that's why I try to spread the least information around me.

I started consuming manga and anime just recently. My favorite mangaka so far is Tsukumizu. Although his art and story are mostly pessimistic, I find myself enjoying his way of talking to the reader as if he knew them since forever. Both of his work were heart-touching to me, especially Shimeji Simulation.

I barely listen to music, as you might have seen from my last.fm page, but I am a big fan of the british band Jamiroquai. Along with Acid Jazz, one of the genres I recently started listening more and more is Math Rock and Drum and Bass, without excluding some Japanese bands of different genres, like frederic or well-known Vocaloid producers.

Projects

Most of the repositories I have contributed to are related to both Android Open Source Project(s) and GNU/Linux, or adding localization to my native language for different projects and more. I always try my best to create something useful and adaptable for everyone and their needs.

  • dumpyara is one of my most active projects right now. It's used by different Android maintainers for automatically extracting important partitions, useful during development.
  • www is where I push changes of my own website, for which I have been dedicating a lot of time to, although it's arguable look. But, after all, I've wrote that on my own, and I'm happy with the outcome.
  • I also have some contributions in different community-driven projects like LineageOS and helluvaOS. Some of these commits had caught the interest of other developers, making me proud of them.
